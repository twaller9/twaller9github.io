01 — Introduction

What Is MedEdAI?

MedEdAI is a startup building AI-powered clinical simulations for medical education. The main idea is that instead of memorizing static case studies, students interact with realistic patient scenarios driven by large language models, receiving adaptive feedback in real time.

This enables students to practice with realistic, real-time communication scenarios without taking the time of another educator or physician. It's purpose is to be a supplement to human-to-human simulations, and to be customizable for almost any medical domain.

02 — My Role

What I Built

My role was working on full-stack development of the application, including API development, frontend, and AI integration. My responsibilities included taking and implementing user feedback, implementing RAG and customizing the AI pipeline for different use cases, and building a functional website to host it all. I am at least partially involved in most aspects regarding development, and I am usually given general and broad tasks to do over time, with expectation of updates and communicaiton.

03 — What Was Hard

Technical Challenges

No project survives first contact with reality. Here are the three problems that required the most attention.

Challenge 01

AI Hallucination

Without proper context and guardrails, the AI chatbot can sometimes dive too deep into its role and produce instructions or data outside the scope of the case. In order to ground it, it needs to be scoped properly and provided with ample context and comprehensive instruction on what it can and can't say.

Challenge 02

User Feedback Loops

Receiving feedback and making changes can be difficult, as there are varying amounts of data to work with. Some people are more specific and others less, meaning some issues can get overlooked. Providing a means for structured and detailed feedback is a necessary part of building an app that satisfies as many users as possible.

Challenge 03

Reliability Issues

When an application is reliant on several dependencies, only one outage or incorrect line of code can render whole features — or even the entire project — unusable for a time. Implementing explicit redundancy and proper CI/CD pipelines helped solve these issues and became a key focus afterwards.

05 — Future Direction

How It Shaped My Goals

Working with MedEdAI has confirmed some things I suspected and changed some things I assumed. I have strengthened my interest in working with AI responsibly. Specifically, I have developed an interest in AI alignment. As we offload more and more responsibilities to AI systems, it is imperative to keep them aligned with the correct goals. My experience showed me exactly how hallucinations, sycophancy, and plain inaccuracy can impact the quality of a product.

This experience has shifted my hopeful long-term career path as well as what I will try to seek out for research in graduate school. While I had specific interests and focuses before, I have become more confident and focused on a select few, one of which is AI alignment. Overall, I believe that the skills and experience I have gained will prove valuable in the next stage of my career.
